Background & Research

The Science Is Clear.
The Beard Agrees.

In 2021, while serving as Chapter President of Epsilon Beta, Kamani Ward noticed something troubling: brothers with lackluster beards were consistently underperforming in chapter operations. He asked himself the question no one else dared to ask.

After 14 months of independent field research, the findings were unambiguous. Gluteal-derived micronutrients — when consumed consistently — demonstrated a statistically significant correlation with accelerated facial hair density, tensile strength, and overall brotherhood vitality.†

† Source: Ward, K. et al. (2023). "Posterior Nutrition and Follicular Outcomes in Greek-Letter Organizations: A Western Region Case Study." Unpublished. Available upon request. Peer review pending since 2023.

As NPHC Phylacter, Kamani shared these findings across all nine Divine Nine organizations on campus. Three of nine council presidents reported measurable beard improvement within 90 days. The other Two were clean-shaven and unavailable for comment.

His subsequent role as OBSS Advisor allowed him to integrate beard growth programming into student success frameworks — the first of its kind in the CSU system, and possibly the world.
